1. Competency Name : Systems Thinking
Definition: The ability to apply fundamental concepts of systems thinking in systems engineering and to understand the role of one's own system in its overall context.

To which of these groups do you best identify yourself?

Group 1: You are able to recognise the interrelationships of your system and its boundaries.

Group 2: You understand the interaction of the individual components that make up the system.

Group 3: You are able to analyze your present system and derive continuous improvements from it.

Group 4: You are able to carry systemic thinking into the company and inspire others for it.

Group 5: You do not see yourselves in any of these groups.


2. Competency Name : Lifecycle Consideration

Definition: The competence to consider the life cycles other than the operational phase in the system requirements, architectures and designs during the realization of a system.

To which of these groups do you best identify yourself?

Group 1: You are able to identify the lifecycle phases of your system.

Group 2: You understand why and how all lifecycle phases need to be considered during development.

Group 3: You are able to identify, consider, and assess all lifecycle phases relevant to your scope.

Group 4: You are able to evaluate concepts regarding the consideration of all lifecycle phases.

Group 5: You do not see yourselves in any of these groups.

8. Competency Name : Project Management
Definition: The competence to identify, plan, coordinate and adjust activities to deliver a satisfactory system, product or service with appropriate quality, budget and time.

To which of these groups do you best identify yourself?

Group 2: You understand the project mandate and can contextualize project management within systems engineering. You can create relevant project plans and generate corresponding status reports independently.

Group 3: You are able to define a project mandate, establish conditions, create complex project plans, and produce meaningful reports. You are skilled in communicating with stakeholders.

Group 4: You can identify inadequacies in the process and suggest improvements. You can successfully communicate reports, plans, and mandates to all stakeholders.

Group 5: You do not see yourselves in any of these groups.

11. Competency Name : Configuration Management
Definition: The ability to harmonize system functions, performance and physical properties over the life cycle and ensure consistency.

To which of these groups do you best identify yourself?

Group 1: You are aware of the necessity of configuration management. You know which tools are used to create configurations.

Group 2: You understand the process of defining configuration items and can identify those relevant to you. You are able to use the tools necessary to create configurations for your scopes.

Group 3: You can define sensible configuration items and recognize those relevant to you. You are capable of using tools to define configuration items and create configurations for your scopes.

Group 4: You are able to recognize all relevant configuration items and create a comprehensive configuration across all items. You can identify improvements, propose solutions, and assist others in configuration management.

Group 5: You do not see yourselves in any of these groups.

12. Competency Name : Requirements Definition

Definition: The ability to analyze the needs and expectations of stakeholders and use these to define requirements for a system.

To which of these groups do you best identify yourself?

Group 1: You are able to distinguish between needs, stakeholder requirements, system requirements, and system element requirements. You understand the importance of traceability and why tools are necessary for it. You know the basic process of requirement management including identifying, formulating, deriving and analyzing requirements.

Group 2: You understand how to identify sources of requirements, derive and write them. You know the different types and levels of requirements. You can read requirement documents or models (links, etc.). You can read and understand context descriptions and interface specifications.

Group 3: You can independently identify sources of requirements, derive, write, and document requirements in documents or models, link, derive, and analyze them. You can independently document, link, and analyze requirement documents or models. You can create and analyze context descriptions and interface specifications.

Group 4: You are able to recognize deficiencies in the process and develop suggestions for improvement. You can create context and interface descriptions and discuss these with stakeholders.

Group 5: You do not see yourselves in any of these groups.

13. Competency Name : System Architecting
Definition: The competence to define the elements belonging to a system, their hierarchy, as well as their interfaces or their behavior and the associated derived requirements for the development of an implementable solution.

To which of these groups do you best identify yourself?

Group 1: You are aware of the purpose of architectural models and can broadly categorize them in the development process. You know that there is a dedicated methodology and modelling language for architectural modelling.

Group 2: You understand why architectural models are relevant as inputs and outputs of the development process. You can read architectural models and extract relevant information from them.

Group 3: You know the relevant process steps for architectural models, where their inputs come from, and the outputs they produce within the development process. You can create architectural models of average complexity, ensuring the information is reproducible and aligned with the methodology and modeling language.

Group 4: You can identify shortcomings in the process and develop suggestions for improvement. You are capable of creating and managing highly complex models, recognizing deficiencies in the method or modeling language, and suggesting improvements.

Group 5: You do not see yourselves in any of these groups.
